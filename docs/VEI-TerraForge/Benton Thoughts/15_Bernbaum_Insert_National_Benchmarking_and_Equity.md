# Bernbaum Meeting Insert: National Benchmarking + Equity Proof (How We Say “National Leader” Safely)

## What we **can** claim (defensible, peer-review safe)
**Benton can be described as a national leader** by reporting **percentile performance within a pre-registered “peer county” cohort**, not by claiming an unverifiable “#1 in America.”

**Example statement (template):**
> “Within counties of similar size and sales volume, Benton ranks in the **top X%** for uniformity (COD) and shows **neutral or improving vertical equity** (PRB/PRD), based on standards-aligned ratio study metrics.”

This is the same structure used in serious performance benchmarking across government systems: cohorts, standardized metrics, published rules.

## What we **will not** claim (avoids easy attacks)
- “Benton is #1 nationally” without a reproducible national dataset and cohort rules
- Any equity claim without: sales qualification rules + trimming policy + uncertainty intervals

## Why it matters for the Legislature
1) **Fairness / trust:** better uniformity and reduced vertical bias means fewer “hidden” distributional errors
2) **Budget stability without changing levy authority:** valuation quality improves allocation of the burden; levy policy controls the total dollars
3) **Operational excellence:** continuous QA prevents drift and reduces appeals pressure

## The measurement system (3 signals that matter)
### 1) Level (Are we near 100%?)
- **Median Ratio (MR)** vs target ≈ 1.00

### 2) Uniformity (Is it consistent?)
- **COD** tracks dispersion of ratios around the median

### 3) Vertical Equity (Is it regressive?)
- **PRD**: simple gauge of price-related bias
- **PRB**: slope-style test of bias (preferred for inference), with confidence intervals

## Our publication-grade guardrails (the “don’t get shredded” list)
1) **Pre-register** the cohort definitions and thresholds
2) Use a **single, declared sales qualification rule set**
3) Report **PRD + PRB** with **bootstrap 95% CI**
4) Run sensitivity variants (trim/no-trim/robust PRB)
5) Stratify only when sample size supports it (residential vs non-res; neighborhoods where n is sufficient)

## What’s next (the proof package)
**Deliverables we will produce and can publish:**
- Annual panel (2010–latest): MR, COD, PRD, PRB (+ CI) for Benton + WA peers
- Cohort roster + bins (so “peer set” is auditable)
- Visuals for the briefing:
  - Trend lines with 2016 marker (first full year of new administration)
  - “Caterpillar” dotplot of Benton’s cohort percentile (selected year)
  - Ratio vs value scatter + decile-binned ratios (diagnostic)

## Quick definitions (plain English)
- **MR:** “Are we, on average, valuing close to market?”
- **COD:** “How tight and consistent are valuations across properties?”
- **PRD/PRB:** “Do lower-value homes tend to be assessed higher (regressivity) compared to higher-value homes?”
