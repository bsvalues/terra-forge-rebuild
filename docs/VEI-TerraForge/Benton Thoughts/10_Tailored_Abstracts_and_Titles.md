# Tailored Titles + Abstracts (3 Venues)

## A) IAAO Journal / Fair & Equitable (Practitioner + Standards)
### Working Title Options
1. **Governing Vertical Equity in Mass Appraisal: A Practical Framework with Evidence from Benton County (2010–2024)**
2. **From Ratio Study Compliance to Measurement Governance: Making Vertical Equity Visible and Correctable**
3. **Vertical Equity as an Operating Objective: Sentinel Metrics, Tier Diagnostics, and Audit-Trailed Reconciliation**

### Abstract (IAAO cut) — 190–220 words
County assessors increasingly face demands for proof that assessments are accurate, uniform, and fair across value tiers. While ratio studies are widely produced, vertical equity is often treated as a retrospective compliance statistic rather than a governable operating objective. This article presents a practical governance framework that operationalizes vertical equity within the annual mass appraisal cycle. The approach organizes assessment administration as a measurement system with four auditable modules—Data Hygiene, Sales Validation, Calibration, and Reconciliation—each producing artifacts that make bias discoverable early and corrections traceable. Using published Benton County (WA) ratio-study outputs from 2010–2024, including year-by-year vertical equity indicators (e.g., PRD where available) and supporting narrative extracts, we demonstrate how a county can build a fail-closed governance posture that protects level and uniformity while monitoring tier fairness. We emphasize tier diagnostics (e.g., ratio-by-price deciles) as minimum annual requirements and position PRD as a sentinel that must be interpreted alongside stratification and trimming sensitivity. The result is an implementable blueprint for offices seeking to strengthen fairness and taxpayer trust without disruptive system replacement. We conclude with a county-ready checklist and a reproducibility protocol suitable for peer review and public-facing reporting.

Keywords: mass appraisal, ratio studies, vertical equity, PRD, governance, quality control

## B) Public Administration / State & Local Government Review (Governance + Legitimacy)
### Working Title Options
1. **Assessment as a Governed Measurement System: Operational Controls, Public Trust, and Evidence from Benton County**
2. **How a County Assessment Office Builds Legitimacy: Governance Gates, Transparency Artifacts, and Levy-Aware Communication**
3. **Operational Excellence Under Constraints: A County Case Study of Measurement Governance (2010–2025)**

### Abstract (Public admin cut) — 200–240 words
Local governments rely on property assessment systems to allocate levied revenues across taxpayers, yet public confidence is frequently undermined by confusion between assessed value changes and tax policy outcomes. This case study examines a county assessor’s office through the lens of measurement governance: the organizational controls and reproducible artifacts that ensure accuracy, consistency, and fairness under statutory deadlines and staffing constraints. Anchored in Benton County, Washington, we synthesize longitudinal ratio-study evidence (2010–2024) and levy context (2010–2025) to frame assessment outputs as publicly verifiable measurements rather than discretionary judgments. We propose a transferable operating model—Data Hygiene → Sales Validation → Calibration → Reconciliation—with explicit governance gates and audit trails. We further show how levy-aware communication (“value ≠ taxes”) functions as a legitimacy mechanism, clarifying what assessment administration controls (distribution) versus what elected policy controls (total levy). Rather than claiming causal effects from published summaries, we present an evaluation design that uses interrupted time-series tests around an operational boundary (2016) and a peer-context comparability rubric. The contribution is a replicable governance architecture for county modernization that strengthens accountability while reducing misattribution of taxpayer outcomes.

Keywords: public administration, measurement governance, accountability, legitimacy, property assessment, levies

## C) Appraisal Institute Dissertation Committee (Practice + Rigorous Methods)
### Working Title Options
1. **A Governance Architecture for Mass Appraisal: Audit-Trailed Quality Control from Data Hygiene to Reconciliation**
2. **Toward Reproducible Assessment Administration: Governance Gates for Level, Uniformity, and Vertical Equity**
3. **Mass Appraisal as Measurement Science: Operationalizing Fairness with Reconciliation Rules and Uncertainty**

### Abstract (Dissertation cut) — 230–260 words
Mass appraisal programs are commonly evaluated by ratio studies, yet many jurisdictions treat ratio-study results as retrospective artifacts rather than governable system properties. This dissertation proposes a governance architecture for mass appraisal that treats assessment as a measurement system with auditable inputs and observable outputs. The architecture is organized as a modular stack—Data Hygiene, Sales Validation, Calibration, and Reconciliation—where each module has defined interfaces, produces reproducible artifacts, and is governed by fail-closed quality gates tied to level, uniformity, and vertical equity metrics. The manuscript integrates longitudinal published evidence from Benton County, Washington (ratio-study outputs, 2010–2024) and levy context (2010–2025) to demonstrate how governance artifacts support both operational performance and taxpayer-facing transparency. A central emphasis is vertical equity: the dissertation formalizes a diagnostic-and-inferential approach in which PRD serves as a sentinel paired with tier diagnostics (ratio-by-price quantiles) and, when sales microdata are available, PRB/slope models with bootstrapped uncertainty and robustness checks for trimming, stratification, and time bias. The expected contribution is twofold: (i) an implementable operating architecture that other counties can adopt without disruptive system replacement, and (ii) a research-grade methods appendix specifying model-based vertical equity estimation and reproducibility requirements. The framework aligns with professional standards expectations by making overrides traceable, decisions reviewable, and outputs defensible to peer review and appeals.

Keywords: mass appraisal, governance, ratio studies, vertical equity, PRB, PRD, reproducibility
