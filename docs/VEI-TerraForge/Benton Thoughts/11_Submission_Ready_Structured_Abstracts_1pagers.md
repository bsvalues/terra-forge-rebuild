## A) IAAO Journal / Fair & Equitable — Structured Abstract (Submission-Ready)

**Proposed Title (pick 1):**
1) Governing Vertical Equity in Mass Appraisal: Sentinel Metrics, Tier Diagnostics, and Audit-Trailed Reconciliation (Benton County, 2010–2024)  
2) From Ratio Study Compliance to Measurement Governance: Making Vertical Equity Visible, Correctable, and Repeatable  
3) Vertical Equity as an Operating Objective: A Governance Framework and Evidence from a County Implementation

**Problem / Motivation**  
Vertical equity is widely reported but rarely governed as an operational objective. Many jurisdictions generate ratio-study outputs without an auditable workflow that translates vertical equity signals into controlled adjustments and defensible reconciliation.

**Objective**  
Define and demonstrate a governance framework that operationalizes vertical equity inside the annual mass appraisal cycle using sentinel indicators (PRD) and tier diagnostics, with explicit gates from data hygiene through reconciliation.

**Data & Setting**  
Benton County, Washington; published ratio-study outputs across multiple years (2010–2024) and supporting operational artifacts. The paper explicitly limits claims to Tier-2 comparability until harmonized microdata are available.

**Methods**  
We frame assessment administration as a measurement system. The governance architecture is specified as a modular stack: Data Hygiene → Sales Validation → Calibration → Reconciliation. Vertical equity is monitored via PRD sentinel reporting and tier-based diagnostics (price/value tiers). Sensitivity reporting is required for trimming and stratification choices. Where microdata are available in future work, PRB/slope estimation is planned with stratified and/or cluster bootstraps.

**Results (What is reported now)**  
(1) Repeatable governance design that converts vertical equity signals into auditable actions;  
(2) Transparent “limits of comparison” language that prevents misuse of peer ranks;  
(3) A county-ready implementation checklist that other offices can adopt.

**Practical Implications**  
Provides an implementable governance blueprint: clear gates, artifacts, and review responsibilities that make vertical equity measurable, explainable, and correctable—without overclaiming national ranks.

**Contribution**  
Moves vertical equity from a retrospective statistic to a governed operating objective, with an audit-trail compatible architecture and publishable comparability constraints.

**Keywords**  
mass appraisal; ratio studies; vertical equity; PRD; governance; auditability; reconciliation

## B) Public Administration / State & Local Government — Structured Abstract (Submission-Ready)

**Proposed Title (pick 1):**
1) Assessment as a Governed Measurement System: Operational Controls, Public Trust, and Levy-Aware Communication (2010–2025)  
2) How an Assessment Office Builds Legitimacy: Governance Gates, Transparency Artifacts, and a “Value ≠ Taxes” Public Narrative  
3) Operational Excellence Under Constraints: A County Case Study of Measurement Governance

**Problem / Motivation**  
Property assessment systems are essential public infrastructure, yet trust is undermined by perceived unfairness and confusion between assessed value changes and tax policy outcomes (levies/rates).

**Objective**  
Explain how a county assessment office can strengthen legitimacy through measurement governance—operational controls, transparent artifacts, and levy-aware communication—while meeting statutory deadlines and resource constraints.

**Setting & Data**  
Benton County, Washington; multi-year assessment performance reporting and levy context (2010–2025). Peer comparisons are framed through explicit comparability tiers.

**Methods**  
Mixed-methods case study grounded in measurement governance: (a) specify governance gates and artifacts, (b) quantify multi-year performance and equity signals, (c) connect taxpayer communication to levy decomposition (“value ≠ taxes”). The 2016 boundary is treated as an intervention point (first full year), suitable for interrupted time-series framing with peer-context sensitivity checks.

**Findings (What is reported now)**  
(1) A practical governance architecture that reduces ambiguity in internal decision-making and external explanation;  
(2) A communications mechanism that separates assessment administration from tax policy outcomes;  
(3) A replicable artifact set that supports accountability and reduces controversy risk.

**Policy / Managerial Implications**  
Recommends measurable controls (gates, evidence logs, tier diagnostics) and a public narrative that aligns citizen experience with statutory reality, improving legitimacy without changing tax policy.

**Contribution**  
Introduces a governance-forward model of assessment administration as measurement infrastructure, connecting technical performance to public trust via levy-aware explanation.

**Keywords**  
public trust; accountability; measurement governance; local government; property assessment; levies; legitimacy

## C) Appraisal Institute Dissertation Committee — Structured Abstract (Committee-Ready)

**Proposed Title (pick 1):**
1) A Governance Architecture for Mass Appraisal: Audit-Trailed Quality Control from Data Hygiene to Reconciliation  
2) Toward Reproducible Assessment Administration: Governance Gates for Level, Uniformity, and Vertical Equity  
3) Mass Appraisal as Measurement Science: Operationalizing Fairness with Reconciliation Rules and Uncertainty

**Motivation**  
Mass appraisal programs are typically evaluated via ratio studies, but operational practice often lacks explicit governance gates that connect inputs, modeling choices, and reconciliation decisions to auditable outputs.

**Research Questions**  
RQ1: What governance architecture makes mass appraisal reproducible and auditable across the annual cycle?  
RQ2: How can vertical equity be operationalized as a governed objective alongside level and uniformity?  
RQ3: How should uncertainty be quantified (bootstraps) and communicated to support defensible decisions?

**Data & Materials**  
Multi-year ratio study outputs and administrative artifacts (Benton County as primary case). Planned: standardized sales microdata extract for PRB/slope estimation and robustness testing.

**Methodology**  
Design science + case study: define the module stack, interfaces, artifacts, and governance gates; validate with multi-year performance evidence; specify statistical models for vertical equity (PRB/slope) with stratified/cluster bootstrap uncertainty; include robustness and anti-gaming checks.

**Expected Contributions**  
(1) A concrete governance architecture that can be implemented;  
(2) A reconciliation framework integrating multiple approaches (sales, cost, income) with evidence gates;  
(3) A defensible uncertainty protocol suitable for peer review and professional standards expectations.

**Practical Value**  
A county-deployable playbook: who checks what, when, with what artifacts, and how to document decisions so they remain defensible under appeal and public scrutiny.

**Keywords**  
mass appraisal; governance; ratio studies; vertical equity; reconciliation; reproducibility; uncertainty
